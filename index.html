About Sport

Formula One (F1) is the highest level of international single-seater auto racing, governed by the FIA (Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile). It features high-performance, open-wheel cars competing in a series of Grand Prix races worldwide on circuits and city streets. Teams, backed by major manufacturers, develop cutting-edge technology to achieve maximum speed and efficiency. The sport is known for its intense competition, strategic pit stops, and elite drivers like Lewis Hamilton and Max Verstappen.
The F1 season culminates in a world championship for both drivers and constructors.

Most Popular Circuits

Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps

Spa-Francorchamps is a legendary Formula 1 track in Belgium, known for its long straights and iconic Eau Rouge turn. It spans 7.004 km with 19 turns. The lap record (1:41.252) was set by Lewis Hamilton in 2020. The track has hosted races since 1925, combining speed and technical complexity.

Circuit de Monaco

Circuit de Monaco is a world-famous Formula 1 street track known for its tight corners, elevation changes, and the iconic Fairmont Hairpin. It spans 3.337 km with 19 turns. The lap record (1:12.909) was set by Lewis Hamilton in 2021. Hosting races since 1929, it’s one of the most challenging circuits on the F1 calendar.

Silverstone

Silverstone Circuit, located in the UK, is one of Formula 1’s most historic tracks. It spans 5.891 km with 18 turns and is known for its high-speed corners like Maggots and Becketts. The lap record (1:27.097) was set by Max Verstappen in 2020. Hosting its first F1 race in 1950, Silverstone is home to the British Grand Prix.

Best Drivers

Michael Schumacher

Michael Schumacher is a legendary German Formula 1 driver, widely regarded as one of the greatest in the sport’s history. He won seven World Championships (1994, 1995, 2000-2004), holding the record for most titles until 2020. Schumacher achieved 91 race victories and 68 pole positions during his career. Known for his fierce competitiveness and technical mastery, he drove for Benetton and Ferrari, leading Ferrari to its most dominant era in the early 2000s. His remarkable consistency and ability to perform under pressure made him a global icon in motorsport.

Max Verstappen

Max Verstappen is a Dutch Formula 1 driver and two-time World Champion (2021, 2022). Driving for Red Bull Racing, he is known for his aggressive driving style and racecraft. Verstappen became the youngest F1 race winner in history at 18 years old (2016 Spanish Grand Prix). By 2023, he had over 50 race wins and multiple pole positions, dominating the sport with Red Bull’s highly competitive car. His rivalry with Lewis Hamilton in 2021 was one of the most intense in F1 history, culminating in a dramatic final race victory in Abu Dhabi.
